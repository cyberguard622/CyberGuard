Cyber Safety Checklist

Practical rules to secure devices and accounts against unauthorized access.

Credential Integrity

  • Generate memorable, long passphrases.
  • Implement unique keys for every platform.
  • Enable Multi Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Manage secrets via trusted managers.

Secure Navigation

  • Analyze URLs carefully before interaction.
  • Decline untrusted file downloads.
  • Enforce HTTPS standard connections.
  • Disregard diagnostic popup warnings.

Network Boundaries

  • Maintain locked private profiles.
  • Evaluate privacy settings periodically.
  • Reject requests from unverified users.
  • Restrict personal structural data sharing.

Device Hardening

  • Apply system patches immediately.
  • Enforce PIN or biometric locks.
  • Execute regular cold system backups.
  • Keep device antivirus definitions active.

Core Threat Vectors

Understanding real world risks aids in prevention and identification.

Social Engineering & Phishing

Malicious actors manipulate communication vectors to masquerade as administration, teachers, or banking support, prompting victims for credential or OTP data.

False Urgency Deceptive Linkage Spoofed Addresses

Authentication Fraud

Tricking users into sharing temporary access tokens, utilizing malicious QR codes in physical spaces, or launching fake shopping templates designed to capture financial parameters.

Token Harvesting QR Exploits Credential Stuffing

Inappropriate Online Conduct

Harassment, cyberbullying, or online impersonation. Ensure you capture screenshots, block offenders, and notify school administration or counselors immediately.

Preserve Evidence Restrict Profiles Report Behavior

Digital Footprint & Privacy

Any item uploaded can persist permanently. Mitigate overexposure by validating software permissions, reducing profile transparency, and vetting connected apps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers regarding standard digital safety issues on our campus.

How can I recognize a phishing email?

Look for mismatched domain names, unusual greeting formats, highly urgent tone, and links directing you to unfamiliar domains.

What makes a passphrase strong?

Length is the key metric. Linking four or five simple random words (e.g., "blue ocean pencil chair") makes a passphrase highly secure yet straightforward to recall.

What happens after submitting a security report?

All inquiries are systematically reviewed by the school systems administrator. Critical security cases are escalated immediately for network intervention.
